What do Americans think of President Biden's State of the Union?

Authors: Brian Chang & Richard Green


A Nation Divided?

There has been growing political division between Democrats and Republicans, the two major political parties in the United States, the past decade. At a time in which Republicans are pushing to reverse Roe V. Wade, block gun control, and continue gerrymandering, it is paramount that Democrats and President Biden earn votes on the campaign trail leading into the 2022 mid-term elections and the 2024 presidential election. To gain insight into how Americans feel about current domestic and foreign issues, we performed sentiment analysis of Tweets about the 2022 State of the Union (SOTU).


Sentiments in North America


We performed sentiment analysis of Tweets queried from the Twitter API if they had mention of #SOTU, #StateoftheUnion, or "Biden", between 2022-03-01 00:00:00 to 2022-03-07 11:59:00. Queried Tweets could not be retweets, must have a corresponding geotag, and written in English. Sentiment analysis was performed with an out-of-the-box transformer model (Hugging Face). The default sentiment analysis pretrained model was used. Text input to the model were not further cleaned. Each corresponding sentiment label ("POSITIVE" or "NEGATIVE") has a corresponding score. For both labels, scores < 0.90 were re-labeled as "NEUTRAL". The model classified majority of Tweets as "NEGATIVE", followed by "POSITIVE", and lastly "NEUTRAL".
